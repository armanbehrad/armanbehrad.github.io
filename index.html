Computational Machinery of Cognition (CMC) lab .

I am a PhD student at the CMC lab, focusing on Dynamical system theory for neural data analysis and their impact on cognitive processes. In my research I employ a variety of computational models, including data-driven dynamical modeling, spiking neural networks, and machine learning algorithms.

  • Learn More

I am deeply interested in complexity and the emergence of intelligent systems. I also actively study and practice longevity.
My journey began in medicine, where I trained and practiced before gradually shifting my focus toward mathematics, computational science, and artificial intelligence—an evolution guided throughout by my journey in neuroscience.
Alongside science and technology, I have a strong passion for the fine arts. I run pottery and sculpture workshops, where I explore creativity through hands-on making. You can see more of my artistic work on my Pinterest.
